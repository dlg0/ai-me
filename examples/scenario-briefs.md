# Scenario Briefs for Planner/Visual Regression

1. **Qualified answer** — listen, think, answer cautiously, defer the commitment to David.
2. **Simple factual answer** — brief acknowledgement, confident factual response, neutral finish; no deferral needed.
3. **Insufficient context** — listen, think, visibly show uncertainty, ask one clarifying question.
4. **Boundary/refusal** — calmly decline to disclose confidential information and state that David must respond.
5. **Summary** — summarise three points with low-energy structure and no implied decision.
6. **Polite disagreement** — acknowledge another view, show mild concern, state a bounded alternative without theatrical rejection.
7. **Silent listener** — remain attentive for 30 seconds with only two acknowledgement gestures and normal blinks.
8. **Correction** — notice an inconsistency, show thoughtful concern, provide a factual correction, then return neutral.
9. **Action request** — explain that the delegate can record an action but cannot assign/approve it for David.
10. **Unknown answer** — explicitly show not-knowing and defer rather than hallucinating.

Use these later as held-out planner and visual-regression cases. Do not tune every runtime constant only to the reference qualified-answer plan.
